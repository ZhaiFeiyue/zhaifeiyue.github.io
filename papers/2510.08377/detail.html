UniVideo: Unified Understanding, Generation, and Editing for Videos

Cong Wei, Quande Liu, Zixuan Ye, Qiulin Wang, Xintao Wang, Pengfei Wan, Kun Gai, Wenhu Chen | 2025-10 | https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.08377 Category: algorithm | Tags: video-generation, multimodal, unified-model, diffusion-transformer, in-context-learning Read: 2026-04-16

Core Contribution

UniVideo proposes a dual-stream architecture (frozen MLLM + MMDiT) that unifies video understanding, generation, and editing under a single multimodal instruction paradigm, using a 3-stage training strategy and demonstrating zero-shot generalization to unseen tasks and task compositions.

核心三问

Q1: 这篇论文试图解决什么核心痛点/问题？

现有统一多模态模型仅能处理文本+图像的理解与生成，无法扩展到视频领域。视频生成模型各自为政（T2V、I2V、视频编辑各需独立模型），且依赖简单文本编码器，无法理解复杂多模态指令（如包含多张参考图+视频的编辑请求）。

Q2: 作者提出了什么新的"杀手锏"方法/架构？

UniVideo 提出双流(dual-stream)架构：MLLM(Qwen2.5VL-7B)负责理解多模态指令 + MMDiT(HunyuanVideo-13B)负责视频生成。通过MLP连接器对齐两个流，并用3阶段训练策略统一T2V、I2V、上下文视频生成、上下文视频编辑、图像编辑。关键创新：使用MMDiT的self-attention（而非cross-attention）整合MLLM特征，只需训练MLP就能实现高效对齐。

Q3: 最终效果/结论如何？

UniVideo在视频理解(MMBench 83.5, MMMU 58.6)和生成(VBench T2V 82.58)上均达到竞争力水平。上下文视频生成的人类评估主体一致性(SC=0.88 single-ref)大幅超越Kling1.6(0.68)和Pika2.2(0.45)。更重要的是，UniVideo展现出零样本泛化能力——未经自由形式视频编辑训练却能执行材质更改、环境变换等未见任务。

逻辑故事还原

背景 (Context)

统一多模态模型在文本+图像领域取得进展（如GPT-image-1、OmniGen2、BAGEL），但视频领域仍是空白。现有视频模型存在三个缺陷：

  1. 各任务需独立模型（T2V、I2V、编辑各一个），维护成本高
  2. 仅用文本编码器处理指令，无法理解复杂多模态输入（如多张参考图+参考视频+编辑指令）
  3. 视频编辑依赖任务特定模块（如mask输入、bias embeddings），扩展性差

    4. 破局 (Insight)

    作者的关键洞察：MLLM擅长理解但不擅长精细视觉生成，而MMDiT擅长生成但不擅长多模态推理——两者互补。

    👉 就像一个影视团队中，导演（MLLM）负责理解剧本需求、解读分镜头、协调各方；而视觉特效师（MMDiT）负责根据导演的指示产出高质量视觉内容。两者通过简单的"翻译器"（MLP连接器）沟通，各司其职又协同工作。更妙的是，使用self-attention让导演和特效师"坐在同一张会议桌上"直接对话，比通过传话筒（cross-attention）效率更高。

    拆解 (Deconstruction)

    1. MLLM理解流: Qwen2.5VL-7B接收文本+图像+视频输入，提取最后一层hidden states作为语义特征
    2. MLP桥接: 可训练的MLP连接器（4×维度扩展）将MLLM特征映射到MMDiT的embedding空间
    3. MMDiT生成流: 同时接收两路信号——MLLM语义特征（理解分支，通过self-attention）+ VAE编码的视觉信号（生成分支，保留精细细节）
    4. 多条件处理: 多个视觉条件（参考图+参考视频）经VAE编码后，padding到统一形状，沿时间轴拼接，使用3D位置编码区分条件/噪声latent
    5. 三阶段训练: Stage 1仅训练MLP连接器（15K步，冻结MLLM+MMDiT）→ Stage 2微调MLP+MMDiT（5K步）→ Stage 3多任务训练（15K步，加入编辑/上下文生成任务）

      关键细节与启示

      技术细节补充:

      1. 3D位置编码 vs MRoPE: 对于区分条件latent和噪声latent，UniVideo使用3D位置编码（保持空间索引不变，仅递增时间维度），比Qwen2-VL的MRoPE（每引入新视觉输入就偏移所有轴）更有效。
      2. MLLM全程冻结: MLLM在三个训练阶段都保持冻结，避免生成任务损害理解能力，同时也使得文本生成能力得以保留。这使UniVideo成为一个"后训练"统一系统而非从头训练的统一模型。

        3. 一句话总结: UniVideo通过"MLLM理解+MMDiT生成"的双流架构和三阶段训练策略，首次将视频理解、生成和编辑统一到一个多模态指令框架中，并展现出向未见任务零样本泛化的能力。

        Summary

        Unified multimodal models have made progress in text-image understanding and generation, but extending this to the video domain remains largely unexplored. Existing video models are task-specific (separate models for T2V, I2V, editing), rely on simple text encoders that cannot handle complex multimodal instructions, and require task-specific modules that limit scalability.

        UniVideo addresses this by combining a frozen Multimodal LLM (Qwen2.5VL-7B) for instruction understanding with a Multimodal DiT (HunyuanVideo-13B) for video generation. The MLLM's last-layer hidden states are projected through an MLP connector into the MMDiT's understanding stream, while visual signals are encoded via VAE into the generation stream. A 3-stage training strategy (connector alignment → fine-tuning → multi-task) enables the model to handle T2V, I2V, in-context video generation, in-context video editing, and image editing from a single model.

        Experiments show UniVideo matches or surpasses task-specific baselines across benchmarks, achieving 0.88 subject consistency in single-reference in-context generation (vs. 0.68 for Kling1.6). Notably, UniVideo demonstrates zero-shot generalization: it transfers editing capabilities from image editing data to free-form video editing and supports novel task compositions without explicit training.

        Key Findings

        • MMDiT self-attention alignment is superior to cross-attention for connecting MLLM with diffusion generator — only MLP training needed
        • Multi-task training significantly improves over single-task baselines (PF +0.16, SC +0.11 average)
        • Dual-branch visual input is critical — removing visual input to MMDiT causes SC to drop from 0.78 to 0.18
        • Zero-shot generalization from image editing to video editing emerges from unified training
        • 3D positional embeddings outperform MRoPE for distinguishing condition vs. noisy latents
        • UniVideo is the only model supporting understanding + image/video generation + image/video editing + in-context generation simultaneously

        Limitations

        • Sometimes over-edits unrelated regions, failing to strictly follow editing instructions
        • Struggles to fully preserve motion of original videos due to backbone limitations
        • Free-form video editing success rate lower than image editing
        • The model is an assembled system (post-trained on frozen MLLM) rather than a native end-to-end multimodal model
        • Limited evaluation on longer video generation tasks

        Infrastructure Impact

        • Algorithm: Direct contribution — new paradigm for unified multi-task video model training; systematic study of MLLM-DiT alignment strategies (self-attention > cross-attention)
        • Kernel: N/A — no custom kernel innovations; uses standard attention and MLP operations
        • Framework: Serving dual-stream MLLM+MMDiT requires handling variable multimodal inputs and conditional VAE encoding; frameworks need to support dynamic sequence lengths from multi-condition concatenation
        • LLM: Demonstrates effective strategy for leveraging frozen MLLMs as multimodal encoders for generation; the MLP connector approach with self-attention alignment is a reusable design pattern for MLLM-DiT integration
        • Agent: Visual prompting capability enables agents to interpret hand-drawn annotations/diagrams for video generation, reducing need for precise text prompts; could power next-gen creative agents
        • Cluster: N/A — no cluster-level optimizations; standard distributed training

        Key differences:

        • Self-attention vs. Cross-attention: UniVideo uses MMDiT's self-attention for MLLM-DiT conditioning (tokens participate jointly). This is more expressive than cross-attention and critically allows alignment with only MLP training. Ablation shows cross-attention variants (a, b) have poor text-following even with full DiT unfreezing.
        • Frozen MLLM: Unlike end-to-end approaches (Emu3, Show-o2), UniVideo freezes the MLLM to preserve understanding capability. This prevents the well-known problem of generation training degrading understanding performance.
        • No task-specific modules: VACE uses heavy adapters, UNIC uses task-specific bias embeddings. UniVideo requires zero task-specific customization — all tasks unified through natural language instructions + 3D position encoding.

        2. Problem Formulation

        Objective: Standard diffusion training loss (flow matching / rectified flow on HunyuanVideo backbone):

        $$\mathcal{L} = \mathbb{E}_{t, \epsilon} \| \text{MMDiT}(z_t, c_{\text{MLLM}}, c_{\text{VAE}}, t) - \epsilon \|^2$$

        Where:

        • $z_t$: noisy video latent at timestep $t$
        • $c_{\text{MLLM}}$: MLLM hidden states projected through MLP connector
        • $c_{\text{VAE}}$: VAE-encoded visual conditions (reference images/videos)
        • Diffusion timestep shift = 5.0

        Assumptions:

        • MLLM's pretrained representations are rich enough to capture multimodal semantics without fine-tuning
        • Self-attention in MMDiT can effectively fuse heterogeneous features (semantic from MLLM + visual from VAE)
        • Multi-task joint training is beneficial (not harmful) for individual tasks

        Prior failure modes addressed:

        • Cross-attention DiT cannot align well with MLLM features (Figure 5 shows poor prompt following)
        • Learnable query approaches (MetaQueries) create capacity bottleneck for video inputs
        • Semantic-only encoding (GPT-image-1 style) loses fine-grained visual details needed for editing

        3. Method Core

        Algorithm (3-stage training):

        
Stage 1: Connector Alignment (15K steps)
  - Freeze MLLM, Freeze MMDiT
  - Train MLP connector only
  - Data: T2I, T2V, image reconstruction
  - LR = 1e-4, resolution: 240-480px, frames: 1

Stage 2: Fine-tuning (5K steps)
  - Freeze MLLM
  - Train MLP + MMDiT
  - Data: high-quality T2I, T2V
  - LR = 2e-5, resolution: 480-854px, frames: 1-129

Stage 3: Multi-task Training (15K steps)
  - Freeze MLLM
  - Train MLP + MMDiT
  - Data: T2I + T2V + I2V + MultiID2V + ID-V2V + Image Editing
  - LR = 2e-5, resolution: 480-854px, frames: 1-129

        Key insight: MMDiT's self-attention architecture enables MLLM alignment with minimal trainable parameters. The MLLM tokens are simply concatenated into the MMDiT's token sequence and interact through self-attention — no architectural modification to either model.

        Hyperparameters:

        • Optimizer: AdamW (β₁=0.9, β₂=0.95, ε=1e-15)
        • EMA ratio: 0.9999 (Stages 2-3)
        • Gradient clip: 1.0
        • Warmup: 50 steps per stage
        • Diffusion timestep shift: 5.0

        Complexity: Inference requires forward pass through MLLM (~7B) + iterative denoising through MMDiT (~13B). The MLLM pass is one-time per generation; MMDiT runs for each denoising step. UniVideo (query) variant reduces MMDiT conditioning tokens to fixed 512 (vs. variable-length all hiddens).

        5. Convergence & Stability

        • No convergence guarantees provided (empirical approach)
        • Stage 1 uses higher LR (1e-4) for MLP-only training; Stages 2-3 use 2e-5 for MMDiT fine-tuning
        • Frozen MLLM is key for stability: prevents catastrophic forgetting of understanding capabilities and provides stable gradient signals to the connector
        • EMA (0.9999) in Stages 2-3 for smoother model averaging
        • Known failure mode: occasional over-editing of unrelated regions, suggesting the model sometimes lacks precise spatial grounding
        • The short total training (35K steps) suggests the method converges quickly, likely due to strong pretrained initializations of both MLLM and MMDiT

        6c. Strength & Weakness Analysis

        Strengths:

        • In-context generation (SC) is dramatically better (+29% over Kling1.6 for single-ref), showing the dual-stream design excels at identity preservation
        • Competitive on in-context editing despite being the ONLY mask-free approach — all baselines require explicit masks
        • Understanding scores match frozen MLLM backbone (Qwen2.5VL-7B), confirming no degradation from generation training

        Weaknesses:

        • VBench T2V (82.58) lags behind Wan2.1 (84.70) — the backbone (HunyuanVideo) is somewhat dated vs. latest T2V models
        • Delete task has lowest scores (PSNR 17.98 vs. VideoPainter 22.99) — hardest editing subtask for mask-free approach
        • No evaluation on longer video generation (>129 frames) or higher resolution (>854px)

        Suspicious results: None detected. The honest reporting of lower delete scores and the acknowledgment of limitations are positive signals.

        Missing benchmarks: No evaluation on video understanding benchmarks (e.g., VideoMME, MVBench) — only image understanding shown.

        AgentVisual prompting (Figure 3, 9) enables agents to use hand-drawn diagrams as video generation instructions, opening new interaction modalities beyond text.
        HardwareTotal inference requires ~20B params (7B MLLM + 13B MMDiT). MLLM is single-pass; MMDiT runs per denoising step. Memory dominated by MMDiT during generation.

        8. Reproducibility

        • Code released: Yes — https://github.com/KlingTeam/UniVideo
        • Model released: Yes
        • Backbone availability: Qwen2.5VL-7B (open-source), HunyuanVideo-T2V-13B (open-source)
        • Training compute: Not explicitly stated, but 35K total steps on 13B model is moderate (~days on 64 GPUs)
        • Data: Internal datasets for T2I/T2V/I2V not released; editing data construction pipeline described but not released
        • Reproducibility assessment: Architecture and training recipe are fully reproducible with open backbones, but the training data (especially internal datasets) is the main bottleneck for full reproduction

        9. Position in Research Landscape

        Most related prior works:

        1. OmniGen2 (Wu et al., 2025) — Unified image understanding and generation but no video support; UniVideo extends to video
        2. VACE (Jiang et al., 2025) — Video creation and editing with adapter design; UniVideo eliminates task-specific adapters
        3. UNIC (Ye et al., 2025) — Unified in-context video editing with task-specific bias; UniVideo removes bias embeddings and adds understanding capability
        4. MetaQueries (Pan et al., 2025) — Frozen MLLM + learnable queries for generation; UniVideo shows hidden states > queries for video tasks
        5. Qwen-Image (Wu et al., 2025) — Dual-stream MLLM+DiT for images; UniVideo generalizes to video

          6. Next logical steps:

          1. Native end-to-end training (not post-trained on frozen MLLM) for deeper understanding-generation synergy
          2. Scaling to longer videos (>129 frames) and higher resolution
          3. Large-scale video editing datasets to improve free-form editing success rate
          4. Stronger video backbones for better motion preservation
          5. Integration with audio generation for unified audiovisual content creation

            6. Open problems:

            1. How to maintain understanding quality when training MLLM end-to-end with generation?
            2. Can the self-attention alignment pattern scale to even larger models (70B+ MLLM)?
            3. How to achieve precise spatial grounding without mask inputs for video editing?
            4. Can this paradigm extend to real-time interactive video editing?
